The Katana

Katana are prestigious swords that were the primary weapon of the samurai of feudal Japan. These highly coveted weapons were at the center of Japan's culture since ancient times. Katana were made by hand using secret techniques that resulted in a sword that was of exceptional quality and beauty. Even today, collector's seek these swords out and some sell for millions of dollars.

Katana are truly works of art. These swords often took months to forge, assemble, and polish. Each part of the construction process was meticulously crafted by Japanese swordsmiths who spent a lifetime mastering the art of making swords.

Katana were not only incredible weapons and works of art but were the focal point of a distinguished lifestyle of honor and prestige. To the samurai of ancient feudal Japan, the katana was a symbol of a lifetime dedication to the art of war. The katana was the weapon of choice to the samurai and all samurai were masters at using the razor sharp, lightweight sword to deliver a swift death to those that opposed them.

In modern times there are several martial arts that use the katana as part of its core theory and practice. Some of these martial arts include Kendo, Iaido, Iaijutsu, Kenjutsu. Other Martials that use the katana or a variation of the katana are Ninjitsu, Aikido, and Jujutsu. The art of making and using katanas is still a highly refined discipline.

The Japanese forging process involves many steps and is a skill that takes one many years to master. To the Japanese swordsmith, forging a katana is a ritual that requires much dedication and strict adherence to centuries of tradition. In short, the blade is their religion and their way of life.

Follow one of these incredible artists as they forge a katana using traditional techniques and materials to bring life to a handcrafted katana of modern times.
